ABOUT

More info

I've always been curious about biology. Even as a child, I was fascinated by how the human body works and what happens inside our cells when disease develops. That curiosity eventually became a career dedicated to understanding the biology behind human disease.

I began my academic journey with a Bachelor's degree in Biology, followed by a research Master's degree in Applied Biotechnology, and later earned a PhD in Biomedical and Clinical Sciences. Along the way, I developed experience in both wet-lab and dry-lab research, combining molecular biology techniques with bioinformatics and genomics to answer complex biological questions.

My research focuses on the molecular mechanisms underlying cardiovascular and pulmonary diseases, with a particular interest in genomics and epigenetics. I'm interested in understanding how diseases develop at the molecular level and using that knowledge to identify new biomarkers and potential therapeutic targets that can ultimately improve patient care.

I enjoy working at the intersection of experimental biology and computational science. Whether I'm generating data in the lab or analyzing large genomic datasets, I like connecting different approaches to better understand disease biology.

Beyond research, I'm passionate about medical writing and scientific communication. I enjoy translating complex scientific findings into clear, engaging, and accessible content for researchers, healthcare professionals, and wider audiences.

This portfolio is a collection of my research, publications, projects, and writing, and reflects my journey as a scientist who loves both discovering new knowledge and sharing it with others.
